Energy Stats is a browseable website for exploring electricity-grid and energy-transition data by country. It uses high-quality public datasets, but it should not be treated as an authoritative reference: figures can be revised by the underlying publishers, methods differ by source, and any summaries here are for exploration rather than formal reporting.

Data sources #

This project draws from multiple organizations. Because methodologies can differ, the site avoids directly combining or comparing metrics from different sources as if they were identical.

  • Energy Institute (EI) — Statistical Review of World Energy: primary energy supply and related energy-system totals. (link)
  • Ember — Monthly Electricity Data: monthly electricity generation, demand, and emissions time series. (link)

Primary energy: interpretation caveats

“Primary energy” is a useful high-level metric, but it can be easy to misread—especially when comparing fossil fuels (often counted as thermal inputs) with non-fossil electricity (often counted as electrical outputs). This can make low-carbon sources appear smaller in “primary energy” terms than they are in delivered electricity.

For a clear explanation of direct vs substituted primary energy (and why the choice matters), see: Our World in Data — What’s the difference between direct and substituted primary energy?
